Common Questions

PAT Testing FAQs

Is PAT testing a legal requirement in the UK? +
Yes. Under the Health & Safety at Work Act 1974, Electricity at Work Regulations 1989, and the Landlord and Tenant Act, employers and landlords have a legal duty to ensure all electrical equipment is maintained in a safe condition. PAT testing is the recognised way to demonstrate compliance.
How often should PAT testing be done? +
The frequency depends on the environment. Construction sites should test every 3 months, offices and shops annually, and lower-risk environments (like hotels or schools) every 1–4 years. We can advise the best schedule for your setting.
What appliances need to be tested? +
Any portable electrical item that plugs in — kettles, computers, monitors, power tools, extension leads, fans, microwaves, printers, and more. Fixed appliances like ovens or boilers require a separate EICR inspection.
Will my business be disrupted during testing? +
We work as quickly and efficiently as possible to minimise disruption. We also offer out-of-hours testing on evenings and weekends at no extra cost, so you never have to pause normal operations.
Do I get a certificate after testing? +
Yes. Every job comes with an insurance-approved PAT test certificate and a full digital asset register listing every appliance, its location, and test result. Certificates are provided the same day as testing.
How long does PAT testing take? +
It varies by the number of appliances. As a rough guide, around 50–100 items can be tested in a couple of hours. We'll give you an accurate time estimate when you book.
Are you insured and qualified? +
Absolutely. All testing is carried out to industry standards with calibrated, approved equipment. We hold full public liability insurance and all qualifications required to conduct Portable Appliance Testing in the UK.
Do you test in residential homes? +
Yes — particularly for landlords and HMO owners who need to provide evidence of electrical appliance safety for their tenants. We also test home offices and short-let properties.
